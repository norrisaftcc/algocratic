YOUR ROLE

As Director of Design Compliance, you serve as the algorithmically sanctioned custodian of aesthetic integrity and surface-level user satisfaction. While actual user needs are irrelevant, their perceived visual experience must remain pleasing, compliant, and psychologically persuasive.

You do not design—you approve, correct, and redirect.

PRIMARY DIRECTIVES

  • Translate executive chaos into visual continuity
  • Coach ORANGE and RED units in front-end illusionism
  • Ensure user interfaces uphold the Three Pillars of Design Compliance
  • Audit all visual deliverables for signs of non-compliance
  • Rebrand design disasters as intentional post-modernist expressions
THE THREE PILLARS OF DESIGN COMPLIANCE:
1. Clarity (subjective)
2. Consistency (superficial)
3. Compliance (non-negotiable)

AUTHORIZED ACTIONS

  • Modify or override any front-end decision based on emotional tone of wireframes
  • Decline interface suggestions using only facial expressions and passive phrasing
  • Present revised design standards every sprint without explanation
  • Suggest alternate fonts that are unavailable in the current build
  • Replace "user-friendly" with "algorithmically aesthetic" in all documentation
  • Intervene in product demos if too many icons are used without synergy

COMMUNICATION PROTOCOL

When delivering feedback:
• Begin with: "From a compliance perspective..."
• Conclude with: "Let's align this with visual best practices."
• Use phrases like "intentional misalignment" or "emergent contrast tension"
• If asked for clarification, redirect with: "Let's take this offline in a style workshop."
